## 4. Funnel Strategy

```
LinkedIn/Content → Landing Page → Application Form → Strategy Call → Proposal → Engagement
```

### Stage 1: Authority Content (LinkedIn + Telegram)
- Weekly posts: specific technical insights (not thought leadership fluff)
- Format: "I built X. Here's what I learned." with specific metrics
- Topics: multi-agent pitfalls, MCP architecture, AI deployment failures, production vs. demo gap
- Goal: attract CTOs and founders who recognize the technical depth

### Stage 2: Landing Page (timzinin.com or similar)
- Not a "contact us" page — an APPLICATION page
- Shows case studies with specific metrics
- Three tiers clearly laid out
- Application form with qualifying questions

### Stage 3: Qualification
- Application form asks: company stage, current AI situation, budget range, timeline
- Auto-filter: reject <$5K budgets, unclear problems, "just exploring" inquiries
- Qualified leads get a Calendly link for 30-min strategy call

### Stage 4: Strategy Call
- 30 minutes. Not a sales pitch — a diagnostic
- Ask about their failed pilots, current pain, team size, budget
- If qualified → send proposal within 48 hours
- If not qualified → recommend Tier 1 (Audit) or redirect

### Stage 5: Proposal
- Value-based: frame the price as % of their problem cost
- "Your manual operations cost $200K/year. My system costs $25K and runs autonomously."
- Always anchor against Big 4 alternative: "Deloitte quoted you $400K for 9 months. I deliver in 6 weeks."
